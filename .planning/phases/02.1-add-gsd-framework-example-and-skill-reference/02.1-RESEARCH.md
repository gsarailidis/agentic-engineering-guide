## Summary

Phase `02.1` should not behave like a second rewrite of the guide. Phase 2 already established the generic vocabulary: environment, artifacts, workflows, delegation, and verification. The planning move here is to cash that out in one concrete framework example. GSD should be introduced as a layer built on those primitives, not as a new conceptual foundation. That keeps the guide coherent and satisfies the requirement to make planning, execution, validation, workflow control, and maintenance explicit.

The strongest structure is one dedicated GSD section with two internal modes. First, a short guided walkthrough explains the main lifecycle skills a reader needs to understand the framework shape at a glance. Second, a full categorized reference lists the installed GSD skills with short descriptions. This preserves readability: the reader gets the system story before the inventory. It also avoids the main failure mode, where the section collapses into a long command directory or quasi-product documentation.

The key planning constraint is proportionality. The GSD section must feel like a concrete worked example of the patterns already taught, not like a new handbook bolted onto the end. Every paragraph should answer one of three questions: what GSD is, how it maps to the Phase 2 primitives, and what the skill catalog reveals about explicit workflow control.

**Primary recommendation:** Add one integrated GSD section that starts with a short lifecycle walkthrough of the core skills, then follows with a categorized full reference grouped by workflow function.

## Common Pitfalls

### Pitfall 1: Letting The Skill Catalog Replace The Explanation
**What goes wrong:** The section becomes a long inventory with little explanation of why GSD exists.
**Why it happens:** The skill list is concrete and tempting to dump directly.
**How to avoid:** Require the section to open with framework framing and a short lifecycle walkthrough before the full reference appears.
**Warning signs:** The first mention of GSD is immediately followed by dozens of skill names.

### Pitfall 2: Turning The Section Into Product Documentation
**What goes wrong:** The guide starts teaching commands, usage flags, or operator workflows in detail.
**Why it happens:** GSD is concrete, and concrete systems invite procedural explanation.
**How to avoid:** Keep every paragraph anchored to the guide's main question: what explicit workflow controls this framework adds on top of a local agent environment.
**Warning signs:** The section answers "how do I run this?" more often than "what operating pattern does this make explicit?"

### Pitfall 3: Weak Editorial Grouping
**What goes wrong:** Skills are grouped arbitrarily or by naming similarity, so the reference is harder to scan than the raw list.
**Why it happens:** A long catalog encourages superficial grouping.
**How to avoid:** Use a small number of lifecycle-based categories with one-line intros that explain why those skills belong together.
**Warning signs:** Readers cannot infer the framework shape from the headings alone.

### Pitfall 4: Repeating Phase 2 Instead Of Extending It
**What goes wrong:** The section spends most of its space re-defining artifacts, skills, and workflows instead of showing how GSD organizes them.
**Why it happens:** The writer tries to make the section standalone.
**How to avoid:** Use short callbacks to prior sections, then move quickly into the concrete framework example.
**Warning signs:** Large paragraphs could be moved into Phase 2 without changing meaning.

### Pitfall 5: Over-Indexing On Comparisons
**What goes wrong:** The section drifts into Claude Code vs Codex CLI vs GSD comparisons.
**Why it happens:** GSD is layered on top of the local-agent environment examples already discussed.
**How to avoid:** Use comparison only to clarify layer boundaries: environment-level tools below, workflow framework above.
**Warning signs:** Comparison becomes a subsection with multiple pros/cons rather than a brief clarifying sentence.
