**Gathered:** 2026-03-24

<domain>
## Phase Boundary

This phase writes the substantive `# VI. Spec-Driven Engineering` section of `AgenticEngineeringGuide.md`. Its job is to explain how strong agent work depends on the chain from problem framing to research to workflow design to explicit specs, with specs treated as the central control surface before execution.

This phase should deepen the guide meaningfully, but it should not turn into a build tutorial, an internal framework manual, or a large procedural handbook. It should stay general to terminal-native local-agent engineering and should continue to set up GSD as a final concrete framework example rather than swallowing that later section.

</domain>

<decisions>
## Implementation Decisions

### Section structure
- The section should be optimized for clarity and quick movement through the text.
- It should match the general style of the surrounding guide sections rather than switching to a radically different format.
- It should still go deep, not stay at a shallow overview level.
- A small number of short subsections is preferred if that helps pace and scanability.

### Conceptual emphasis
- The section should cover the full chain: framing, research, workflow design, and specs.
- Specs should be the center of gravity.
- The earlier parts of the chain should mainly matter insofar as they explain where good specs come from and why they matter.

### Concreteness
- Keep the section mostly conceptual.
- Add concrete examples only where the idea would otherwise be difficult to understand clearly.
- Avoid sprinkling examples everywhere just to make the section feel practical.

### Scope discipline
- Keep the section general to local terminal-native agent engineering, not GSD-specific.
- Do not turn the section into a tutorial for writing specs or a checklist-heavy operator guide.
- Do not reopen the already-stabilized introduction-through-Claude-Code spine.

### Claude's Discretion
- Exact subsection names and ordering inside `# VI`
- How to balance prose, bullets, and examples for scanability
- Which concrete examples are necessary versus optional

</decisions>

<specifics>
## Specific Ideas

- The section should feel easy to move through even while going deep.
- It should explain why specs matter as execution contracts rather than presenting them as paperwork.
- Concrete examples should be used sparingly and only to clarify difficult concepts.
