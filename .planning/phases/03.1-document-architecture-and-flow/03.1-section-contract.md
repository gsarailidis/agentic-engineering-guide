# 03.1 Section Contract

This contract translates the approved Phase `03.1` diagnosis into concrete section roles, ordering rules, and scope boundaries for the architecture pass.

The purpose of this file is to keep the refactor narrow but not shallow: fix what blocks coherence, preserve what still works, and defer anything that belongs to Phase `03.2` or Phase `4`.

## Target Top-Level Order

1. `Introduction`
2. `# I. Agentic AI (the behavior)`
3. `# II. Agentic Engineering (the discipline)`
4. `# III. Definitions`
5. `# IV. Agentic Frameworks`
6. `# V. Claude Code`
7. `# VI. Problem Framing, Research, And Spec-Driven Engineering`
8. `# VII. GSD As A Workflow Framework`

This order is intentional. The guide should move from subject and scope, to minimal conceptual prerequisites, to environment-level patterns, to a concrete harness, to the reserved workflow-design layer, and only then to GSD as the final workflow-framework example.

## Section Roles

### Introduction

Job: establish the subject, the scope boundary, and the reading map in one place, including the distinction between development-time terminal-native agent work and production-system agents embedded in shipped systems.

Handoff: send the reader into sections `I-III` knowing they are minimal prerequisites for the environment-first body rather than a generic overview of all agentic systems.

### I. Agentic AI (the behavior)

Job: define the minimal behavioral idea of an agentic system clearly enough that the rest of the guide can talk about loops, goals, tool use, and state without re-defining them later.

Handoff: pass from generic behavior to the engineering problem of making that behavior controllable and useful in real environments.

### II. Agentic Engineering (the discipline)

Job: explain why agentic behavior becomes an engineering discipline once the system has to run through explicit loops, tools, policies, and verification in a real workspace.

Handoff: prepare the reader for environment design by narrowing the section to the concerns that later sections actually instantiate, not by trying to inventory every possible topic.

### III. Definitions

Job: define the vocabulary the guide reuses later, especially harness, tools, skills, and memory, without stealing the explanatory work that belongs to sections `IV-VII`.

Handoff: leave the reader ready for the environment-level treatment in `# IV. Agentic Frameworks`.

### IV. Agentic Frameworks

Job: explain the environment-level patterns of terminal-native local-agent systems: workspace surfaces, operating loops, artifacts, reusable workflows, delegation, and bounded verification/recovery.

Handoff: move from general environment patterns to one concrete harness that makes those patterns visible.

### V. Claude Code

Job: serve as the concrete harness example that makes the abstract environment contract legible through filesystem access, shell execution, structured tools, permissions, artifacts, and delegation.

Handoff: show that the guide can now move one layer up from harness behavior to workflow design and workflow control.

### VI. Problem Framing, Research, And Spec-Driven Engineering

Job: reserve the top-level workflow-design slot for Phase `03.2` and explain only the bridge: why framing, research, workflow design, and specs belong after the harness example but before the final workflow-framework example.

Handoff: point forward to fuller Phase `03.2` content while also preparing the reader to understand GSD as one concrete workflow framework layered on top of the harness.

### VII. GSD As A Workflow Framework

Job: present GSD as the final concrete workflow-framework example, showing how artifacts, planning, execution, validation, and continuity are organized on top of the local-agent environment.

Handoff: close the guide's current argument by showing one structured workflow layer above the environment and harness layers.

## Scope Discipline

Phase `03.1` may strengthen only the explanations that the diagnosis identified as coherence blockers:

- the introduction-level development-time versus production-system distinction
- the introduction as a reading map instead of only a topic list
- the handoff from sections `I-III` into the environment-first body
- the section boundaries around loop, control, harness, and workflow structure
- the missing top-level bridge before GSD

Phase `03.1` must not:

- draft the substantive Phase `03.2` body
- rewrite sections `I-III` wholesale unless a diagnosed blocker makes a local structure unusable
- add new framework comparisons, new major examples, or broad surveys
- pull final compactness work from Phase `4` into this refactor

If a new sentence does not directly support coherence, it is out of scope for this phase.

## Guardrail Reconciliation

Phase `03.1` explicitly authorizes targeted edits before `# IV. Agentic Frameworks` because the approved diagnosis identified real coherence problems in the introduction and sections `I-III`.

The old full-prefix freeze from Phase `1` is historical rather than authoritative for this architecture pass. It captured a previous boundary decision, but it does not override the later approved scope of Phase `03.1`.

The practical rule for this plan is:

- edits before `# IV. Agentic Frameworks` are allowed when they are justified by the approved diagnosis
- edits must stay tightly bounded to the section-role contract above
- the refactor must not be used as cover for broad expansion

After the structural refactor lands, the project will create a new phase-local snapshot and verifier that reflects the stabilized boundary for subsequent work.

This contract does not draft any substantive Phase `03.2` content. It only reserves that slot and defines why it belongs there.
